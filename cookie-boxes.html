SNICKERDOODLE

Yield: 24 cookies

Time: About 45 minutes

INGREDIENTS

  • 3 ½ cups/ 445 grams all-purpose flour
  • 1 ½ teaspoons cream of tartar
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 cup/ 225 grams unsalted butter (2 sticks), at room temperature
  • 1 ¾ cup/ 350 grams granulated sugar, plus ¼ cup/ 50 grams for rolling
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1 large egg plus 2 yolks, at room temperature
  • 1 tablespoon rum extract
  • 2 teaspoons freshly grated nutmeg

PREPARATION

STEP 1

In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, cream of tartar and baking soda, and set aside. In the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with the paddle attachment, cream together the butter, 1 3/4 cups sugar and the salt on medium-high until very smooth and fluffy, 3 to 4 minutes.

STEP 2

Scrape down the sides of the bowl, return the mixer to medium speed and add the egg and egg yolks, incorporating each before adding the next. Add the rum extract. Scrape down the sides of the bowl and continue to beat for another minute or two.

STEP 3

Add the flour mixture and beat on low speed until combined. (You may want to start by “pulsing” the flour, so that it doesn’t go everywhere.) Set the dough aside to hydrate for about 10 minutes while you prepare to bake.

STEP 4

Heat oven to 350 degrees and line 2 rimmed baking sheets with parchment paper. Make the nutmeg-sugar mixture by combining the remaining 1/4 cup/50 grams sugar with the nutmeg in a small bowl.

STEP 5

Roll the dough into balls, each one the size of a golf ball or about 45 grams, then roll each dough ball in the nutmeg-sugar. Transfer the balls to the baking sheets, about 2 inches apart, and bake for 10 to 13 minutes, rotating pans and switching racks halfway through, until slightly puffed and just set.

STEP 6

Let the cookies cool slightly on the baking sheet before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely. These cookies are even better the day after they're made, and will keep for 4 days sealed in an airtight container.

BROWNIE SHORTBREAD

Yield: 32 bars

Time: 1 ½ hours, plus cooling

INGREDIENTS

FOR THE SHORTBREAD:

  • 1 ½ cups/ 340 grams cold unsalted butter (3 sticks), cut into 1/2-inch pieces, plus more for greasing the pan
  • 3 cups/ 385 grams all-purpose flour
  • ¾ cup/ 150 grams granulated sugar
  • 1 ¼ teaspoons fine sea salt

FOR THE BROWNIES:

  • 1 cup plus 2 tablespoons unsalted butter (2 1/4 sticks)
  • 3 ounces unsweetened chocolate, chopped
  • 1 ¼ cups/ 265 grams light brown sugar
  • 1 cup/ 200 grams granulated sugar
  • ½ cup plus 1 tablespoon/ 45 grams cocoa powder
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract
  • 1 ½ cups/ 190 grams all-purpose flour
  • ¼ teaspoon fine sea salt
  • ¾ cup/ 90 grams slivered almonds, chopped walnuts or pecans (optional, we used almonds)
  • Flaky sea salt

PREPARATION

STEP 1

Heat oven to 350 degrees. Grease a 9-by-13-inch baking dish, and line with parchment paper so that there is a 2-inch overhang on the two long sides.

STEP 2

Prepare the shortbread: In the bowl of an electric mixer fitted with a paddle attachment or beaters, mix together flour, sugar and salt. Beat in butter on low speed until dough just comes together but is still a little crumbly. (Or pulse together ingredients in a food processor.)

STEP 3

Press dough into prepared pan. Prick dough all over with a fork. Bake until golden, 30 to 35 minutes. Remove from oven. Raise oven temperature to 375 degrees.

STEP 4

As shortbread bakes, prepare the brownie: Place butter and chocolate in a large bowl. Melt in the microwave in 30-second bursts, stirring after each burst, until smooth. (Alternatively, place bowl over a pot of simmering water, and heat chocolate and butter, stirring until smooth and melted.) Whisk in sugars and cocoa powder until smooth, then whisk in eggs and vanilla.

STEP 5

In a medium bowl, whisk together flour and sea salt. Whisk into chocolate mixture until no streaks of flour remain. Fold in nuts, if using. Spread mixture onto the warm shortbread base. Sprinkle lightly but evenly with flaky sea salt.

STEP 6

Bake until the top is set, the center is soft, and the edges start pulling away from the pan, 23 to 28 minutes. (A toothpick inserted into the center will come out gooey.) Transfer to a wire rack to cool completely. Cut into bars before serving.

CINNAMON RUGELACH

Yield: 4 dozen cookies

Time: 1 hour 55 minutes

INGREDIENTS

  • 8 ounces cream cheese, at room temperature
  • ½ pound (2 sticks) unsalted butter, at room temperature
  • ¼ cup granulated sugar plus 9 tablespoons
  • ¼ teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • ¼ cup light brown sugar, packed
  • 4 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1 egg beaten with 1 tablespoon milk, for egg wash

PREPARATION

STEP 1

Cream the cheese and butter in the bowl of an electric mixer fitted with the paddle attachment until light. Add 1/4 cup granulated sugar, the salt, and vanilla. With the mixer on low speed, add the flour and mix until just combined. Dump the dough out onto a well-floured board and roll it into a ball. Cut the ball in quarters, wrap each piece in plastic, and refrigerate for 1 hour.

STEP 2

To make the filling, combine 9 tablespoons of granulated sugar, the brown sugar, and the cinnamon.

STEP 3

On a well-floured board, roll each ball of dough into a 9-inch circle. Sprinkle the dough with slightly less than 1/4 of the filling. Press the filling lightly into the dough. Cut the circle into 12 equal wedges, cutting the whole circle in quarters, then each quarter into thirds. Starting with the wide edge, roll up each wedge. Place the cookies, points tucked under, on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Chill for 30 minutes.

STEP 4

Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F.

STEP 5

Brush each cookie with the egg wash. Combine 3 tablespoons granulated sugar and 1 teaspoon cinnamon and sprinkle on the cookies. Bake for 15 to 20 minutes, until lightly browned. Remove to a wire rack and let cool.

CHOCOLATE CHIP COOKIE BRITTLE

Yield: 20-25 pieces

Time: About 45 minutes

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup/ 225 grams unsalted butter (2 sticks), at room temperature
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 ½ teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups semi-sweet morsels
  • 1 cup chopped nuts (we used a mix of almonds and cashews)

PREPARATION

STEP 1

Preheat oven to 375 degrees F.

STEP 2

Beat butter, sugar, vanilla and salt in a large bowl.

STEP 3

Gradually beat in flour. Stir in morsels and nuts until a dough forms. Mixture may be crumbly.

STEP 4

Press smooth into an ungreased 9” x 13” pan. (You can optionally line with parchment paper first).

STEP 5

Bake for 20 - 25 minutes until golden brown and set.

STEP 6

Cool until just slightly warm.

STEP 7

Break cookies apart into irregular pieces.

NOTE

Cookie Brittle freezes well. Caution - don’t break a tooth eating it straight out of the freezer! :)

BREAD PUDDING

Yield: 12 servings

Time: 4 hours

INGREDIENTS

FOR BREAD PUDDING:

  • 1 panettone or other fruit-studded sweet bread (about 1 pound), cut into 1-inch slices
  • 1 quart half-and-half
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 tablespoon plus 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon pure almond extract

FOR AMARETTO SAUCE (makes 1 cup):

  • 1 stick (4 ounces) unsalted butter, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup confectioners’ sugar
  • 3 tablespoons amaretto
  • 2 egg yolks

PREPARATION

STEP 1

Preheat the broiler. Arrange the bread slices on 2 baking sheets and toast 2 inches from the heat for about 1 minute a side, or until lightly browned. Set aside for at least 1 hour.

STEP 2

Tear the toasted panettone into 1 ½-inch pieces and place in a large bowl. Pour the half-and-half over the bread, making sure all the pieces are moistened. Set aside for 1 hour until all the liquid has been absorbed, tossing ½ way through.

STEP 3

Preheat the oven to 325 degrees F. Generously butter the bottom and sides of a 9” x 13” x 2” baking dish and set aside. In a medium bowl, whisk the eggs with the sugar, vanilla and almond extracts until blended. Stir into the soaked bread. Transfer the mixture to the prepared baking dish and bake in the middle of the oven for about 1 hour, or until the pudding is set and the top is golden brown. Let cool to room temperature. (The pudding can be prepared 1 day ahead. Cover and set aside at room temperature).

STEP 4

Make the Amaretto Sauce. In a medium bowl set over a small saucepan of simmering water or in the top of a double boiler, melt the butter. Slowly whisk in the sugar until the mixture is creamy, about 30 seconds. Add the amaretto and then the egg yolks, one at a time, whisking constantly. Cook, whisking constantly until the sauce is the consistency of honey and reaches 160 degrees F, about 4 minutes. Let cool to room temperature. (The recipe can be made up to 1 day ahead. Cover and refrigerate. Let return to room temperature before using).

STEP 5

Preheat the broiler. Just before serving, spoon the Amaretto Sauce over the pudding and broil 3 to 4 inches from the heat until bubbly and lightly browned, about 2 minutes. Cut the bread pudding into 12 rectangular pieces and serve immediately.
