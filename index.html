Amani Ariel

Amani Ariel (born to the name Amani Hayes-Messinger) is working on her first full length manuscript, and has essays and articles published in African Voices Magazine and on Blavity.com. She writes, journals, and thinks about Black mothering, evolving definitions of family, who has the right to decide how family is defined, and more. In her work, she explores what it means to be the adult child of two women, what it means to be donor conceived, and the tensions of biology and legality as a Black, queer, Jewish woman raised by two moms.

Amani is an avid reader, a stress baker, and can spend hours in a kitchen store. She lives, hosts, and plays word games in Brooklyn with her fiancée.
